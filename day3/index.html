Reading Time: 5 Minutes

The History

Leonardo da Vinci is 30 years old. He is talented, but he has a reputation: He never finishes anything. He leaves Florence for Milan to find a new patron, Duke Ludovico Sforza.

The Power Shift

Leonardo writes the famous "Sforza Resume." He lists 10 skills to get the job.

Number 1-9 are about war: "I can build bridges," "I can divert rivers," "I can make armored tanks."

Only at number 10 does he add: "Also, I can paint."

The Duke hired him. Not as a painter, but as a military engineer and party planner.

Reading Time: 5 Minutes

The Scandal

The Duke asked Leonardo to paint the dining hall of a monastery.

Normal "Fresco" painting (painting on wet plaster) requires speed. You have to finish before the plaster dries.

Leonardo hated speed. He wanted to think, stare, and layer.

The Failure

So, Leonardo invented a new mix of oil and tempera to paint on dry wall. It allowed him to take his time.

It was a disaster. The paint didn't bond. Within 20 years, the greatest painting in the world began to flake off the wall. Today, only about 20% of the original paint remains. It is a masterpiece that is slowly dying.

Reading Time: 10 Minutes

The Work: The Last Supper

The Artist: Leonardo da Vinci

Visual Decoding

This isn't a dinner party; it is an explosion.

Leonardo captured the specific second after Jesus said: "One of you will betray me."

Look at the reaction of the disciples. They are grouped in threes. They are terrified.

Jesus forms a calm triangle in the chaos.

Your "Cocktail Party" Fact

The prior of the monastery complained that Leonardo was working too slowly. Leonardo told the Duke: "I am searching for a face evil enough to model for Judas. If I can't find one, I will use the face of the prior." The prior stopped complaining.
