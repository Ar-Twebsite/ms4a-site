Day 7: The End of the World

üìú Part 1: The Context (The Crash)

Reading Time: 5 Minutes

The History

It is 1527. The High Renaissance is dead.

Why? Because foreign armies (The Holy Roman Empire) sacked Rome. They looted the city, killed priests, and stabled horses in the Vatican.

The optimism is gone. The Church is under attack by Martin Luther and the Protestants.

The Power Shift

Art changes. It stops being perfect and pretty. It becomes twisted, dark, and chaotic. This style is called "Mannerism."

Reading Time: 5 Minutes

The Scandal

Michelangelo is called back to the Sistine Chapel 25 years after he finished the ceiling. He is an old man now. The Pope asks him to paint the wall behind the altar.

He paints The Last Judgment. It is terrifying. Everyone is naked and muscular.

The Pope's Master of Ceremonies, Biagio da Cesena, complained: "This is a bathroom scene, not a chapel."

The Burn

Michelangelo painted Biagio into Hell (bottom right corner). He gave him donkey ears (for being an ass) and had a snake biting his genitals.

Biagio complained to the Pope. The Pope laughed and said: "I have authority in Earth and Heaven, but I have no jurisdiction in Hell. You stay there."

üé® Part 3: The Masterpiece (The Flayed Man)

Reading Time: 10 Minutes

The Work: The Last Judgment

The Artist: Michelangelo

Visual Decoding

Look at Jesus in the center. He isn't the skinny, suffering Jesus. He is massive, like a bodybuilder. He looks angry. He is smiting the damned.

The Self-Portrait:

Look at Saint Bartholomew (to the right of Jesus). He was martyred by being skinned alive, so he holds his own skin.

Look closely at the face on the limp skin.

It is a self-portrait of Michelangelo.

He felt flayed by the church, by his art, and by his life. It is the saddest selfie in history.
