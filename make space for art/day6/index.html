The History

While Michelangelo was grumping around in the Sistine Chapel, a young, handsome, charming guy arrived in Rome.

Raphael Sanzio.

The Power Shift

Everyone loved Raphael. He was polite, he dressed well, and he was fast. The Pope loved him.

While Michelangelo was painting the ceiling, the Pope hired Raphael to paint his private library (The Stanza della Segnatura) right down the hall.

The architect Bramante (who had the keys) let Raphael sneak in.

Raphael looked up and was stunned by the massive figures Michelangelo was painting.

Reading Time: 10 Minutes

The Work: The School of Athens

The Artist: Raphael

Visual Decoding

This painting represents "Philosophy." It brings together all the great thinkers of history into one room.

The Center:

Plato (left) points UP (to the ideal world/heaven). He looks like Leonardo da Vinci.

Aristotle (right) points DOWN (to the earth/science).

Your "Cocktail Party" Fact

Raphael painted himself in the corner. On the far right, there is a young man looking directly at you. That's him. Breaking the fourth wall.
