Reading Time: 5 Minutes

The History

Yesterday, under the Medici, Florence was a 24/7 party of philosophy and art. But parties end.

By the late 1490s, the bank was losing money, and the people were hungry. When people are hungry, they get radical.

The Power Shift

Enter Girolamo Savonarola. He wasn't a banker; he was a fanatical monk.

He looked at the art, the makeup, and the poetry, and he didn't see beauty. He saw corruption. He preached that the Medici were leading Florence to Hell.

His sermons were terrifying. When the French army invaded (by coincidence), the people believed Savonarola was a prophet. They kicked the Medici out. Florence went from a carnival to a monastery overnight.

Reading Time: 5 Minutes

The Scandal

Savonarola organized the "Bonfire of the Vanities" (1497).

He sent boys door-to-door to collect "vanities."

What is a vanity? Mirrors, cosmetics, fancy clothes, musical instruments, and art.

They built a pyramid in the city square, 60 feet high, and set it on fire.

The Tragedy

Sandro Botticelli, the sensitive artist who painted the famous Birth of Venus, fell under Savonarola‚Äôs spell.

Botticelli was terrified for his soul. According to biography, Botticelli himself threw some of his own paintings onto the fire. The "Golden Age" was burned to ash by the very people who created it.

Reading Time: 10 Minutes

The Work: The Birth of Venus

The Artist: Sandro Botticelli

Visual Decoding

This painting survived the fire.

Look at Venus (the center). She is floating on a shell. She is nude.

This was shocking. For 1,000 years, the only female nude allowed in art was Eve (shameful). Botticelli made nudity divine again.

She isn't standing correctly (she has no weight); she looks like she is cut out of paper. This wasn't a mistake‚Äîit was a style choice to show she belonged to a spiritual world, not the physical one.

Your "Cocktail Party" Fact

Savonarola eventually went too far. He annoyed the Pope. The Pope excommunicated him. The people of Florence turned on the monk, and a year after the Bonfire, they burned Savonarola in the exact same spot he burned the art.
